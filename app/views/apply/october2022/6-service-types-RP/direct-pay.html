How you get child maintenance

The Child Maintenance Service has two service types. After you submit your application, we will tell you which service type you’ll use and how much child maintenance you should get.

Direct Pay

Direct Pay is a free service that you manage using your online child maintenance account.

The other parent will need your bank or building society details to make payments to you. If you do not want to have any contact with the other parent, we can pass your details on for you.

If the other parent does not pay, you can use your online child maintenance account to:

  • tell us if payments are not made in full or on time
  • report missed payments
  • request to change to the Collect and Pay service

Collect and Pay

The Child Maintenance Service will:

  • collect payment from the other parent and pass it to you
  • manage payments for you
  • take action to recover missed payments

There are extra fees for this service:

  • 20% is added to the amount the other parent pays
  • 4% is taken off the amount you get

For example, if the payment amount was £100, the other parent would pay £120 and you would get £96.
